This idea holds that test count is a vanity metric: the real measure of an
experimentation capability is whether the organization knows more than it did
last quarter, and can prove it.

It develops across the three-pillar model — a trustworthy platform, a governed
program, and an embedded culture — and the claim that value is created or
destroyed in the overlaps between them.
